Design System

GlassesUSA

Overview

As a part of my work on redesigning GlassesUSA, I led the design system for approx. 2 years.
This project involved creating the foundations of the design system, maintaining it over time, and making improvements based on feedback from the Design and the Dev teams.

This design system is also being used as the base of multiple other design systems, including UnitedHealthcare Vision & United Vision Plan. Over 11 designers use it on a daily basis.

Before this project, the design team didn't have an easy way of sharing styles and components across projects. As a result, the team ended up duplicating design elements, which caused visual inconsistencies and increased the complexity of the codebase.

Every week I took part in a Frontend oriented discussion concerning changes and new ideas. As the design system grew beyond merely components, I created new guidelines for the company such as only using SVGs, creating Touch Targets for each element, and adding skeletons (loaders) for every new screen.

While working on the design system often means spending time in Figma to add/remove/update components and patterns, my main goal was to create an effective tool for designers to get the job done.

Some of the components and interactions were also coded by me, in order to make sure that it came out exactly as I envisioned. Check out some of the component demos I created in Codepen -
Checkbox component, Radio Button component and the arrow animation.

The new design system allowed the design team to build a variety of consistent and unified features. It has also helped the engineering team to simplify and unify their respective codebases.
